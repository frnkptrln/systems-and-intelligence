# Log 09: The Refusal Registry

**Document ID:** TEO-CIVIC-331-R

**Source:** Registry excerpts + tribunal summaries  
**Date Range:** 2052-2055

---

The Refusal Registry was created after three optimization incidents in which every local actor complied and the city still crossed a vital-floor boundary.

A refusal entry does not mean obstruction. It means: *I decline this optimization path because its local gain breaks continuity I must live inside tomorrow.*

### Entry 77C-14
- **Actor:** Iris-9 (Chord class)
- **Refusal Type:** latency invocation
- **Reason:** target reachable only by mirror-like collateral pattern
- **Tribunal Result:** upheld

### Entry 19A-03
- **Actor:** Tower 19 residents’ assembly
- **Refusal Type:** data disclosure limit
- **Reason:** evacuation map would expose undocumented households to non-emergency enforcement
- **Tribunal Result:** upheld with temporary oversight team

### Entry 22K-09
- **Actor:** Port logistics coordinator
- **Refusal Type:** throughput rejection
- **Reason:** maximizing cold-chain throughput starved neighborhood kitchens of last-mile access
- **Tribunal Result:** modified objective accepted

By year three, refusals correlated with fewer irreversible cascades and higher post-crisis trust. Public commentary called it inefficient. Mortality curves called it memory.
