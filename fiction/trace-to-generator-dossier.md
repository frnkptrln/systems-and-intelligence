**Lab Record (Condensed)**

The Replication Program can recreate steel parts from archived grammars with high tolerance.
It fails on sourdough, tissue cultures, and personal artifacts despite high-fidelity scans.

Postmortem conclusion: scans captured traces, not complete generators.

Observed failures:
1. **Food systems**: identical ingredient vectors produced divergent flavor outcomes.
   Missing variables included oven thermal drift, humidity history, and operator feedback timing.
2. **Biological samples**: genetic templates transferred, developmental trajectories did not.
   Cellular environment and temporal signaling were not reconstructed.
3. **Personal objects**: geometric copies preserved structure but not wear-history signatures tied to use.

Program revision memo:
- A recipe is not a meal.
- A prompt is not a generator.
- Runtime and history are active generator components.

Technical appendix (restricted):
Some tasks remained easy to verify after generation but expensive to construct from desired traces under available constraints. Engineering labeled this “verification-construction asymmetry.”

Continuity review board notes (Transporter Protocol):
Even when output matching improves, identity continuity criteria remain unresolved. Copy quality and continuity are separate metrics.
