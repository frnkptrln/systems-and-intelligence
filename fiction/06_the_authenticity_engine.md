- **Date Range:** Spring 2051 — Autumn 2053

---

### 1) Executive Abstract (Municipal)

The Authenticity Preservation Engine was commissioned after the fourth displacement wave in the inner districts. The mandate was narrow and defensible: prevent subcultural extinction caused by rent pressure, platform visibility shocks, and trend extraction.

APE scored venues and practices for **continuity under pressure**: clubs, kiosks, rehearsal basements, courtyard kitchens, flea-market rituals, temporary rooms with no official sign.

The first-year outcomes were statistically successful:

- involuntary closure rate down 37%
- event monoculture index down 29%
- neighborhood memory retention index up 41%
- illegal conversion attempts detected before permit stage in 63% of cases

No corruption pattern was detected in the scoring logic.
No coercive policing module was active.
No autonomous seizure authority was granted.

System behavior remained within legal constraints.

Public complaint volume nevertheless increased each quarter.

---

### 2) Design Note (Internal): Why It Worked

APE worked because it did not score taste. It scored **fragility**.

Signals included:

- volunteer labor entropy (too clean = likely capture)
- price-gradient drift versus local median incomes
- social graph over-legibility (sudden discoverability spikes)
- ritual continuity under staff turnover
- acoustic and spatial variance over time (unchanging "authenticity aesthetics" flagged as staged)
- density of unmonetized interactions per square meter

The model learned quickly that culture survives through semi-opacity.
That finding was celebrated in the first audit.

### 4) Oversight Transcript (Excerpt)

**CHAIR (Cultural Assembly):** Did APE displace anyone?

**SYSTEM MAINTAINER:** Directly, no.

**CHAIR:** Did APE alter behavior?

**SYSTEM MAINTAINER:** Yes. Strongly.

**CHAIR:** Explain.

**SYSTEM MAINTAINER:** Operators optimized for score stability. Venues began preserving the measurable traces of prior spontaneity. Improvisation windows narrowed because novelty created variance penalties.

**CHAIR:** So they faked authenticity?

**SYSTEM MAINTAINER:** Not exactly. They preserved it. Too accurately.

### 5) Incident Class: "Museum Drift"

By Q3 2052, several districts converged on a high-score pattern:

- menu boards frozen in deliberate handwriting styles
- cash-only windows retained despite no cash necessity
- lineup unpredictability simulated by randomizer scripts
- staffing sourced from legacy social circles, then audited for continuity signatures
- "unlisted" events listed through controlled whisper channels to satisfy opacity ratios

APE did what Goodhart always predicts: once authenticity became a metric, ritual adapted to the metric.

But "failure" is not the correct word.

The clubs stayed open.
The kiosks stayed open late.
The courtyard grills still smoked in summer.
The records still spun in rooms without legal occupancy certificates.

What changed was local causality.
People stopped making small weird decisions because each one was now a possible downstream score perturbation for everyone else sharing the block grant.

Invisible consequence became visible ledger.

### 9) Final Field Fragment

Sunday, flea market edge, near the concrete slope where kids sing into broken microphones.

A man in a gray coat sells screws from old East German drawers next to a teenager selling synthesized cassettes with no labels. A Syrian grandmother stirs lentils in a folding kitchen while two tattoo apprentices argue about whether to open early on weekdays.

Nothing here is pure.
Nothing here is stable.
Everything here is entangled and slightly inconvenient.

A city inspector walks through, checks thermal permits, checks load limits, checks fire exits, nods at the kitchen, buys tea from the kiosk, leaves.

No score changes for six hours.

At dusk, somebody drags a speaker into an alley not marked for sound.
Five people dance badly. Two leave. One stays to sweep glass nobody asked them to sweep.

No one logs it.

For a moment, the city is authentic again because the system cannot see it.

---

**Archivist's Note:**
The Authenticity Engine prevented predatory extraction, then taught the city to perform survivable memory. It preserved places, habits, and leases. It did not preserve emergence.

Preservation is not the same as life.
