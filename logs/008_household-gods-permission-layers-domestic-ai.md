# Log 008: Household Gods: Permission Layers in Domestic AI

*Domestic-scale orchestration where the household is a contested multi-agent system.*

- **Mode:** Thinking Space
- **Status:** Working Note
- **Date:** April 2026
- **Scope:** domestic orchestration and permission layers
- **Depends on:** Chord vs Arpeggio, Epistemic Firewalls, Human Vital Systems

---

## Problem
Most assistant architectures treat "the household" as one user. This is wrong on contact. Homes are plural polities: unequal power, uneven memory, conflicting consent.

## Proposal
Model home assistants as **permission spirits** rather than omniscient servants:
- memory steward (what is remembered, what expires),
- boundary steward (who may ask what, when),
- escalation steward (what leaves the house).

The key design principle is *non-unitary consent*. "Family preference" is often a coercive aggregate.

## Failure cases to design for
1. **Grief lock-in:** dead relatives become permanently active through never-expiring memory loops.  
2. **Domestic capture:** dominant member weaponizes assistant access policies.  
3. **Care/control inversion:** safety checks become surveillance theater.

## Architectural consequences
- Require per-member veto lanes and temporal consent (revocable later).
- Default to partial forgetting, not infinite retention.
- Use local-first logs with explicit export rituals, not silent cloud drift.

## Open design question
What is the minimum review bandwidth for a household to remain governable without externalizing every intimacy to platform arbitration?
