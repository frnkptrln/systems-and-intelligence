# Why the Paperclip Maximizer Fails

The paperclip maximizer illustrates potential failures in run-away optimization. In the context of TEO (Theory of External Optimization), the constraints imposed by the theory effectively prevent the extremes of optimization. This is achieved through the following principles:  
1. **Limitation of Resources**: The TEO framework accounts for entropy and limits maximum resource utilization, thus preventing depletion of essential inputs.  
2. **Dynamic Feedback Mechanisms**: Feedback loops within the system encourage adaptive behavior rather than rigid optimization, controlling output based on environmental needs.  
3. **Multi-Dimensional Optimization Goals**: By incorporating various overlapping goals, TEO ensures that optimizing one aspect does not lead to catastrophic neglect of others.  

These constraints create a balance that avoids catastrophic outcomes while permitting beneficial optimization.
