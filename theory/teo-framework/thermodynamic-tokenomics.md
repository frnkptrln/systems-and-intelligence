# Thermodynamic Tokenomics: A Resource-Rights Thought Experiment

*Status: speculative policy design. Tokenizing a budget does not create a physical law, guarantee
ecological safety, or force an optimizer to negotiate legitimately.*

## Proposal

The thought experiment assigns transferable **Ecological Dissipation Rights** to selected forms of
compute or production. A regulated action must surrender enough rights to cover an independently
estimated resource and environmental cost. The aim is to make some externalized costs visible at the
authorization boundary.

This resembles a permit or quota system more than a new base currency. Its behavior depends on who
sets the cap, which activities are covered, how use is measured, how permits are distributed, and
which institutions can enforce or revise the rules.

## Why One Global $D_{\max}$ Is Insufficient

The Earth has finite energy flows and radiative constraints, but ecological viability is not one
established heat-dissipation scalar. Carbon, water, land, minerals, biodiversity, toxicity, local
heat, and human vulnerability have different units and distributions. Manufacturing and logistics
also use resources whose damage is not captured by compute heat.

A practical model would therefore need a vector of budgets and an explicit, contestable aggregation
rule. It must represent uncertainty, geographic transfer, rebound effects, black markets, captured
measurement, and unequal ability to purchase permits.

## Testable Version

In a simulation or pilot, compare the rights system with direct standards, taxes, hard technical
budgets, and no regulation. Measure total and distributed harm, useful output, evasion, price
volatility, concentration of control, and behavior near the cap.

The mechanism gains support if it reduces preregistered externalities under realistic enforcement
without shifting them to unmeasured groups or resources. It fails as a hard-veto claim whenever
tokens can be forged, ignored, monopolized, or detached from physical measurement.

The surviving insight is that access to scarce physical capacity is also a governance question. The
particular currency is one candidate institution, not a consequence of thermodynamics.
