# Invariance and Identity (Framing Note)

**Status:** Working Note

**Scope:** Names a concept the repository has been converging on without using the word: invariance — what survives a transformation — as the common structure beneath basis-change, identity persistence, equivalence classes, and the question of what a trace can determine at all.

**Epistemic status:** Framing note over settled mathematics (Klein, Noether) and one philosophical anchor (Nozick); the *mapping* onto the repo's instruments is this project's contribution and is tagged where it claims. Personal origin: a sentence the author has carried since school — *"Ein Stern weicht nicht aus seiner Bahn"* (attributed, with honest uncertainty, to Goethe; the canonical relative is the Zahme Xenien's *"Wie das Gestirn, ohne Hast, aber ohne Rast"*) — once written out in old German script with quill and ink, which is perhaps why it stuck: slow encoding, performed once, in matter.

**Failure conditions:**

## One concept, four costumes

Within a single week, the same structure surfaced four times in this project, each time wearing different clothes:

1. **Linear algebra** (the author's thread): understanding as finding the coordinate system in which structure becomes visible — and the recognition that eigen*values* survive the change of basis while coordinates do not.
2. **Perturbed cognition** ([psychedelics as perturbation](../identity/psychedelics-as-perturbation.md)): a forced change of basis is epistemically valuable exactly insofar as something survives the transformation *back* — insight as that which is basis-independent.
3. **The star** (the personal origin): a star does not hold its orbit by discipline. The orbit is the *expression* of conserved quantities. The mature reading of the schoolboy's sentence: not "never deviate" but **"what is conserved chooses the path."**
4. **Relativity, properly read**: the most misnamed theory in physics. Its content is not that everything is relative but that some things are *absolute* — c, the interval, proper time — and that physics is precisely the invariant content under change of observer. Einstein's 1905 move was invariant extraction at civilizational scale.

The word for all four is **invariance**, and it has a formal home older than any of them.

## The formal anchors

- **Klein's Erlangen Program (1872):** a geometry *is* the study of what its transformation group leaves unchanged. Euclidean geometry is what survives rigid motions; topology is what survives continuous deformation. The author's linear-algebra intuition, scaled to a research program: to understand a domain, name its transformations and study their invariants.
- **Noether's theorem (1918):** under its variational and regularity conditions, a continuous symmetry yields a conserved quantity. This is the star's sentence made rigorous in a specific physical-mathematical setting—not a universal theorem that every persistent agent property has a hidden symmetry.
- **Objectivity as invariance** (Nozick, *Invariances*, 2001; the working ethos of physics since Minkowski): what is objective about a description is what all admissible observers agree on — the invariant content under observer-transformation.

## The repo mapping `[HYPOTHESIZED]`

The instruments this repository already runs are invariance tests that never said the word:

- **Identity Persistence can be tested as invariance under perturbation.** The Chord/Arpeggio question — does a declared property return after a kick? — asks which measured properties persist and which are coordinates of the moment. The transformation family is the perturbation protocol; IP is one proposed instrument. This does not define absolute identity.
- **Identity across recurrent practice is an invariance claim** `[HYPOTHESIZED]`. [From Action to Culture](../emergence/from-action-to-culture.md) proposes that identity is not one repeated act but the higher-order pattern across practices a system reproduces, revises, and refuses. The admissible transformations must be declared by the test—changes of context, time, role demand, tool, or bounded disruption. The candidate invariant is the organization among practices: selection, ordering, composition, conflict resolution, and repair. Repetition without such a protocol does not establish identity.
- **The equivalence class is relative to tests.** What a trace determines is what all trace-consistent candidate process models share under the chosen observation protocol. Rule 90's five observed neighborhoods constrain five rule-table entries; the three unseen bits remain unidentified. The [benchmark](../../lab/benchmarks/inverse-reconstruction/README.md) measures that class and how interventions refine it. Calling every shared feature an invariant requires an explicitly named transformation or equivalence relation.
- **The insight criterion** ([psychedelics note](../identity/psychedelics-as-perturbation.md)): of what a perturbed basis shows, only what survives the transformation back is a candidate for knowledge. Integration as invariant extraction.
- **The mirror, restated:** an impostor can match visible traces while differing under held-out interventions. Those interventions may define a useful identity test; they do not prove that identity *lives* in one privileged invariant family.

One discipline falls out, and it is this note's corrected rule: **never claim an invariant without naming the transformation family, represented quantity, and equality notion.** “X is invariant” is an empty compliment; “the distribution of $q$ is unchanged under intervention family $S$, up to tolerance $\varepsilon$” is a measurement instruction.

## Open threads

- **Noether for agents:** which *symmetries* of an agent's architecture generate its persistent quantities? If IP is a conservation law, what is the symmetry behind it? (Speculative but well-posed; the Chord architecture is the natural testbed.)
- **Time's arrow and identification:** prediction and retrodiction can have different conditioning and computational costs in specified models, but no generic forward/inverse hardness law follows. A relation to the thermodynamic arrow would require an explicit model and is not pursued here.
- **Measure it:** the Identity Suite's metrics could be reorganized explicitly as invariance reports — *property, transformation family, equality/tolerance, preserved or not* — which would make the identity layer's claims mechanically auditable.
