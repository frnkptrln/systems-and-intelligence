# Invariance and Identity (Framing Note)

**Status:** Working Note

**Scope:** Names a concept the repository has been converging on without using the word: invariance — what survives a transformation — as the common structure beneath basis-change, identity persistence, equivalence classes, and the question of what a trace can determine at all.

**Epistemic status:** Framing note over settled mathematics (Klein, Noether) and one philosophical anchor (Nozick); the *mapping* onto the repo's instruments is this project's contribution and is tagged where it claims. Personal origin: a sentence the author has carried since school — *"Ein Stern weicht nicht aus seiner Bahn"* (attributed, with honest uncertainty, to Goethe; the canonical relative is the Zahme Xenien's *"Wie das Gestirn, ohne Hast, aber ohne Rast"*) — once written out in old German script with quill and ink, which is perhaps why it stuck: slow encoding, performed once, in matter.

**Failure conditions:**

- Using "invariant" without naming the transformation group. An invariant is always *invariant under something*; if the something cannot be specified, the word is decoration and must go.
- Letting the physics borrow authority: Einstein and Noether are *receipts* that invariance-thinking works, not claims this repository makes about physics.

## One concept, four costumes

Within a single week, the same structure surfaced four times in this project, each time wearing different clothes:

1. **Linear algebra** (the author's thread): understanding as finding the coordinate system in which structure becomes visible — and the recognition that eigen*values* survive the change of basis while coordinates do not.
2. **Perturbed cognition** ([psychedelics as perturbation](../identity/psychedelics-as-perturbation.md)): a forced change of basis is epistemically valuable exactly insofar as something survives the transformation *back* — insight as that which is basis-independent.
3. **The star** (the personal origin): a star does not hold its orbit by discipline. The orbit is the *expression* of conserved quantities. The mature reading of the schoolboy's sentence: not "never deviate" but **"what is conserved chooses the path."**
4. **Relativity, properly read**: the most misnamed theory in physics. Its content is not that everything is relative but that some things are *absolute* — c, the interval, proper time — and that physics is precisely the invariant content under change of observer. Einstein's 1905 move was invariant extraction at civilizational scale.

The word for all four is **invariance**, and it has a formal home older than any of them.

## The formal anchors

- **Klein's Erlangen Program (1872):** a geometry *is* the study of what its transformation group leaves unchanged. Euclidean geometry is what survives rigid motions; topology is what survives continuous deformation. The author's linear-algebra intuition, scaled to a research program: to understand a domain, name its transformations and study their invariants.
- **Noether's theorem (1918):** every continuous symmetry yields a conserved quantity. This is the star's sentence made rigorous — invariances *generate* the conservation laws that determine orbits. Constancy is not stubbornness; it is symmetry, expressed.
- **Objectivity as invariance** (Nozick, *Invariances*, 2001; the working ethos of physics since Minkowski): what is objective about a description is what all admissible observers agree on — the invariant content under observer-transformation.

## The repo mapping `[HYPOTHESIZED]`

The instruments this repository already runs are invariance tests that never said the word:

- **Identity Persistence is invariance under perturbation.** The Chord/Arpeggio question — does the system return as *itself* after a kick? — asks which properties of an agent are conserved quantities and which are coordinates of the moment. The transformation group is the perturbation protocol; IP measures its invariants. (The star, applied to agents: you may leave the path — play, basis change, intervention — and remain the star, *if* what returns is governed by the same conserved quantities.)
- **The equivalence class is a statement about invariants.** What a trace determines is exactly what all trace-consistent generators *share* — the invariant content of the class. Rule 90's five observed neighborhoods are the invariants; the three unseen bits are coordinate freedom. The [benchmark](../../lab/benchmarks/inverse-reconstruction/README.md) has been measuring class-invariants all along; interventions collapse the class by destroying coordinate freedom, not by adding invariants.
- **The insight criterion** ([psychedelics note](../identity/psychedelics-as-perturbation.md)): of what a perturbed basis shows, only what survives the transformation back is a candidate for knowledge. Integration as invariant extraction.
- **The mirror, restated:** an impostor matches coordinates (the visible trace); identity lives in the invariants (what would be conserved under queries the impostor never faced). Morelli's earlobes were invariants of the master's hand.

One discipline falls out, and it is this note's rule: **never claim an invariant without naming the group.** "X is invariant" is an empty compliment; "X is invariant under Y" is a measurement instruction.
