# What This Project Does NOT Claim

**Status:** Reference — maintained alongside the claims themselves.

**Scope:** The explicit negative space of the repository. Trust in the positive claims is earned by keeping this page accurate; if you find an assertion anywhere in the repo that exceeds these boundaries, that is a defect — please file it.

---

This repository does not claim a theory of everything. It studies one recurring asymmetry: **simple generators produce rich traces cheaply, but reconstructing the generator from its trace is hard — and optimizing systems become dangerous when their constraint architecture cannot scale with their capability.** Everything else is scaffolding, instrumentation, or honest speculation, marked as such.

The specific non-claims, each with the place where the boundary is stated:

1. **We do not claim P ≠ NP.** It is a tagged `[FOUNDATIONAL ASSUMPTION]`, used in its *practical* form — and parts of the framework (equivalence classes, attractor degeneracy) would survive even P = NP, while others would fall. [The Generator Question, Wall 1](../core/the-generator-question.md).

2. **We do not claim inversion is uniformly hard.** Our own benchmark shows the opposite: with a known model family, full observability, and clean data, generator recovery is *cheap*. The hardness lives — measurably — in family search, noise × differentiation, partial observability, and coverage. [Inverse-reconstruction benchmark](../../lab/benchmarks/inverse-reconstruction/README.md).

3. **We do not claim the necessity theorem is deep.** Taken alone it is close to definitional (the viable region is *defined* by the three constraints). It is scaffolding; the substantive, demonstrated content is capability loading and single-axis insufficiency. [The Viable Corridor, §7.1](../../papers/viable-corridor.md).

4. **We do not claim sufficiency.** That the three constraints jointly *suffice* for viability is a `[CONJECTURE]`, expected to require $\gamma > \gamma_c$; it is unproven, and the numerical evidence is single-trajectory, not open-set. [Paper §3.4, Appendix C.5](../../papers/viable-corridor.md).

5. **We do not claim civilization has been measured.** The AI ↔ civilization mapping is a `[HEURISTIC]` regime assignment with stated falsification conditions — not a calibration. $K_c$ for real societies is currently not operationalizable; the measurement debt is documented. [Paper §4, §5.4, §6.3](../../papers/viable-corridor.md).

6. **We do not claim results about real AI agents.** The P7/P8 evidence (hard vs. soft budgets; capability loading) comes from two *synthetic* models. Whether real LLM agent ecologies behave the same way is open and explicitly pending. [Paper Appendix D](../../papers/viable-corridor.md); [benchmark roadmap](../../lab/benchmarks/inverse-reconstruction/README.md).

7. **We do not claim identity *is* generator recovery.** That the trace/generator formalization captures what matters about identity is a second tagged `[FOUNDATIONAL ASSUMPTION]`, with both failure directions stated (identity might need less — or more: embodiment, history, runtime). [The Generator Question](../core/the-generator-question.md); [Log 016](../../logs/016_the-runtime-is-part-of-the-generator.md).

8. **We do not claim anything about consciousness.** Where consciousness-adjacent language appears, it is tagged `[SPECULATIVE]` and reduced to architectural properties (broadcast, integration, perturbation response). [Consciousness as Global Availability](../identity/consciousness-as-global-availability.md); [Limitations](limitations-and-honest-assessment.md).

9. **We do not claim elegance finds truth.** Occam selection is a *prior*: measurably reliable on simple targets, exactly chance on a uniform world, and systematically wrong on complex targets until coverage is near-total. It pays only insofar as the world is biased toward simplicity — an empirical bet. [Family-search testbed](../../lab/benchmarks/inverse-reconstruction/README.md); [Construction vs. Deduction](../computation/construction-vs-deduction.md).

10. **We do not claim the benchmark results generalize beyond their toys.** They are floors and existence demonstrations in small, fully-controlled systems — valuable precisely because everything is checkable there, and not more. [Benchmark caveats](../../lab/benchmarks/inverse-reconstruction/README.md).

11. **We do not treat fiction as evidence.** The fiction layer stress-tests concepts and sometimes generates them; resonance is not evidence, and theory may not inherit authority from a story. [Narrative as Cognitive Technology](../narrative/narrative-as-cognitive-technology.md); [fiction/README](../../fiction/README.md).

12. **We do not claim the framework is finished or externally validated.** The paper is a working draft (not submission-ready); no external domain expert has yet reviewed it; the project's own provenance-depth standard would classify most of its claims as model-mediated, not performed. [Paper status block](../../papers/viable-corridor.md); [Log 017](../../logs/017_provenance-depth-and-the-verification-economy.md).

---

*The pattern behind this page: the repository's credibility strategy is not to claim more, but to make what is claimed checkable — status tags on claims, external anchors on concepts, "what it does NOT show" on simulations, and this page on the whole. If the positive documents ever drift above these lines, this page wins.*
