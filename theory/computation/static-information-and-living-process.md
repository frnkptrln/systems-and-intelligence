# Static Information, Living Process, and the Generator Boundary

**Status:** Working Note

**Scope:** A reading aid, not a theory: four information concepts (Shannon, Kolmogorov, Bateson, computation) placed as rungs of one ladder along the repository's spine, plus one tagged speculative bridge (generator composition / symbiogenesis). Everything here resolves to an existing home or is marked as an open question; the note adds vocabulary discipline, not claims.

**Epistemic status:** The ladder is a `[HYPOTHESIZED]` organizing reading of established formalisms — the formalisms themselves are textbook material; only their *placement* along trace→generator is this repository's. The composition bridge is `[SPECULATIVE]` with external anchors and no in-repo artifact yet.

**Failure conditions:**

- Treating the rungs as rival definitions of one thing. They answer different questions (channel, description, effect, process) and are not in competition.
- Letting any rung imply the next. There is computation without composition, composition without self-binding; the ladder is not an escalator.
- Using BFF-style results as proof of digital life, machine consciousness, or personhood. They are an illustrative experiment family for generator emergence, and nothing more here.
- Introducing vocabulary without a home. Every term below is defined, linked, or explicitly parked as open.

## The ladder

**Shannon: information as resolved uncertainty.** Entropy, channel, mutual information — the mathematics of *traces and their correlations*. It is deliberately semantically neutral: a Shannon bit does not know what it is about, and mutual information between two streams says nothing about which generator coupled them. This is the repository's instrumentation rung ([Axiom 2](../core/mathematical-axioms.md); the [web explorer's](../../lab/tools/README.md) live entropy/MI charts). In spine terms: **a trace is information after the fact.**

**Kolmogorov: information as shortest description.** $K(x)$ moves from the trace toward the generator — but only toward its *description*. Two things keep this rung honest, both already in force: $K(x)$ is uncomputable ([Wall 2](../core/the-generator-question.md)), and a description is **still static** — a program listing produces nothing until it runs. [Generative compression](../emergence/generative-compression.md) is this repository's operational version: what matters is not minimal length but *executable* description that supports prediction, variation, and intervention.

**Bateson: information as "a difference that makes a difference"** (Bateson 1972). The rung where information stops being a property of the signal and becomes a property of a *coupling*: a difference counts as information only relative to a receiving process it can affect. This repository measured exactly that distinction before naming it: passive watching accumulates Shannon-correlations that plateau, while queries create **distinguishing traces** — differences that make a difference to the reconstructor's equivalence class ([measurement note](../core/measurement-as-weak-intervention.md); benchmark v1.1). The Bateson rung is the conceptual name for what the intervention experiments do.

**Computation: a generator unfolding in time.** The static/living boundary of this note's title. A description becomes a process only inside a runtime — and the runtime is then *part of the generator*, not an implementation detail ([Log 016](../../logs/016_the-runtime-is-part-of-the-generator.md)); constructive possession is possession by execution ([construction vs. deduction](construction-vs-deduction.md)). In spine terms: **computation begins when a description is given time.**

Folded into one line: *a trace is information after the fact; Kolmogorov points toward the generator's description; Bateson points toward its effect; computation is the generator living.*

## The composition bridge `[HYPOTHESIZED]` — a first measurement

Higher-order generators may arise not by search but by **composition**: existing generators coupling until the composite produces trace families none of its parts produces alone. Biology's anchor is symbiogenesis (Margulis 1967: the eukaryotic cell as a merger, not a mutation). The computational illustration is the BFF / computational-life family (Agüera y Arcas et al. 2024): in soups of random code, self-replicators *emerge and compose* without any fitness function being asked for them. Agüera y Arcas's own compression of the view — *"life is an ecology of functions"* (spoken formulation, podcast interview, 2026) — is the frame this rung takes: not a single merge event but a standing web of coupled functions. The repository already carries his line elsewhere ([origin of intelligence](../emergence/emergence-origin-intelligence.md), [social computation](../identity/the-non-individual-intelligence.md)); the spine reading this note adds is composition as a way new generators enter the world **without being searched for**.

The project's synthesis of that frame, in the note's own terms:

> *Life is an ecology of functions: not static information, not isolated computation, but a self-maintaining composition of processes whose functions make differences for one another. The generator of life is not a single program, but an ecology of functions that turns information into continuation.*

Every clause resolves to a rung, which is why this is a summary and not a new claim: *not static information* is the Shannon rung declining to be the whole story; *not isolated computation* is the computation rung standing alone declining likewise; *make differences for one another* is the Bateson rung, now **mutual** — each function is both the difference-maker and the affected process; *self-maintaining … turns information into continuation* is the one clause the note cannot yet cash — it names **co-stabilization**, the point at which a composition stops being merely a higher-order generator and begins to hold *itself* in existence. That is the open question below, and it is a real one: v1.8 measured composition, not yet self-maintenance.

That reading now has a first artifact ([benchmark v1.8](../../lab/benchmarks/inverse-reconstruction/README.md)): couple two elementary CAs, observe one stream, and ask which single rule produced it. Composition gets a benchmark-native definition — a generator whose trace **empties the equivalence class** of the component family — and the measurement sharpens the bridge in one move: composition is visible to the observer *exactly* where it makes a trace-difference the observed generator transmits. A center-blind rule (90) hosts an active partner and still reads as a lone generator at every coupling strength; the wall is not the fit but the **level jump** to a family whose description size just multiplied (the [family-search floor](../../lab/benchmarks/inverse-reconstruction/family_search.py), one rung up). The population version — a self-organizing ecology rather than a designed pair — is the open follow-up.

Kept strictly apart: self-replication and takeover are *dynamical* phenomena. They demonstrate generator emergence; they say nothing about experience, and this note draws no line from one to the other.

## Boundary / non-claims

- No claim that BFF-style experiments prove digital life in general, machine consciousness, or AI personhood — they show self-replicators emerging in specific computational substrates, full stop.
- No claim that any current AI system is conscious; consciousness material stays in its two nodes.
- No claim that the ladder is a theory: it is a placement of existing formalisms, useful exactly insofar as it prevents category errors (measuring a trace and calling it a generator; possessing a description and calling it a process).
- Shannon, Kolmogorov, and Bateson are not graded from worse to better. Each is the right tool for its question.

## Open questions

1. **From pair to ecology — co-stabilization as the measurable threshold.** The minimal testbed is [built](../../lab/benchmarks/inverse-reconstruction/composition.py) (v1.8): the empty class certifies a higher-order generator, and visibility tracks whether the observed generator transmits the coupling. The open remainder is *self-maintenance*, and the synthesis statement above names its mechanism — **co-stabilization**: an ecology in which each function holds the others in a viable regime. This is measurable, and by the repository's own hierarchy it is measurable only one way: co-stabilization is invisible to watching (a healthy ecology looks like mere coexistence) and shows only under **knockout** — remove or freeze one function and measure whether the rest degrade *beyond* that function's direct contribution. Additive degradation is coexistence; super-additive collapse is an ecology holding itself up. The design is the biologist's knockout with the benchmark's viability metrics attached; it is the coverage/Bateson principle's would-be fourth appearance (exp6 bindings, exp7 lures, v1.8 symbionts, here dependencies). That experiment is where "ecology of functions" stops being a phrase and becomes a threshold. Unbuilt; the concrete next artifact.
2. **Does the Bateson rung reduce without remainder to intervention-identifiability** (the regime hierarchy), or does "difference that makes a difference" carry content beyond it? If it fully reduces, the term is an external anchor rather than a new concept — worth knowing either way, and decidable by use.
3. **Valence stays outside.** Whether self-binding requires a valence-like signal is untouched by this repository's instruments; the term has no home here and is deliberately not introduced (registry rule: a term with no layer, home, experiment, or open problem is decoration — this entry is its open problem, and no more).
