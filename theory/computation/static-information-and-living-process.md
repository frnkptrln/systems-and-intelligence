# Static Information, Living Process, and the Generator Boundary

**Status:** Working Note

**Scope:** A reading aid, not a theory: four information concepts (Shannon, Kolmogorov, Bateson, computation) placed as rungs of one ladder along the repository's spine, plus one tagged speculative bridge (generator composition / symbiogenesis). Everything here resolves to an existing home or is marked as an open question; the note adds vocabulary discipline, not claims.

**Epistemic status:** The ladder is a `[HYPOTHESIZED]` organizing reading of established formalisms — the formalisms themselves are textbook material; only their *placement* along trace→generator is this repository's. The composition bridge is `[SPECULATIVE]` with external anchors and no in-repo artifact yet.

**Failure conditions:**

- Treating the rungs as rival definitions of one thing. They answer different questions (channel, description, effect, process) and are not in competition.
- Letting any rung imply the next. There is computation without composition, composition without self-binding; the ladder is not an escalator.
- Using BFF-style results as proof of digital life, machine consciousness, or personhood. They are an illustrative experiment family for generator emergence, and nothing more here.
- Introducing vocabulary without a home. Every term below is defined, linked, or explicitly parked as open.

## The ladder

**Shannon: information as resolved uncertainty.** Entropy, channel, mutual information — the mathematics of *traces and their correlations*. It is deliberately semantically neutral: a Shannon bit does not know what it is about, and mutual information between two streams says nothing about which generator coupled them. This is the repository's instrumentation rung ([Axiom 2](../core/mathematical-axioms.md); the [web explorer's](../../lab/tools/web-explorer/README.md) live entropy/MI charts). In spine terms: **a trace is information after the fact.**

**Kolmogorov: information as shortest description.** $K(x)$ moves from the trace toward the generator — but only toward its *description*. Two things keep this rung honest, both already in force: $K(x)$ is uncomputable ([Wall 2](../core/the-generator-question.md)), and a description is **still static** — a program listing produces nothing until it runs. [Generative compression](../emergence/generative-compression.md) is this repository's operational version: what matters is not minimal length but *executable* description that supports prediction, variation, and intervention.

**Bateson: information as "a difference that makes a difference"** (Bateson 1972). The rung where information stops being a property of the signal and becomes a property of a *coupling*: a difference counts as information only relative to a receiving process it can affect. This repository measured exactly that distinction before naming it: passive watching accumulates Shannon-correlations that plateau, while queries create **distinguishing traces** — differences that make a difference to the reconstructor's equivalence class ([measurement note](../core/measurement-as-weak-intervention.md); benchmark v1.1). The Bateson rung is the conceptual name for what the intervention experiments do.

**Computation: a generator unfolding in time.** The static/living boundary of this note's title. A description becomes a process only inside a runtime — and the runtime is then *part of the generator*, not an implementation detail ([Log 016](../../logs/016_the-runtime-is-part-of-the-generator.md)); constructive possession is possession by execution ([construction vs. deduction](construction-vs-deduction.md)). In spine terms: **computation begins when a description is given time.**

Folded into one line: *a trace is information after the fact; Kolmogorov points toward the generator's description; Bateson points toward its effect; computation is the generator living.*

## The composition bridge `[SPECULATIVE]`

Higher-order generators may arise not by search but by **composition**: existing generators coupling until the composite produces trace families none of its parts produces alone. Biology's anchor is symbiogenesis (Margulis 1967: the eukaryotic cell as a merger, not a mutation). The computational illustration is the BFF / computational-life family (Agüera y Arcas et al. 2024): in soups of random code, self-replicators *emerge and compose* without any fitness function being asked for them. The repository already carries the Agüera y Arcas line elsewhere ([origin of intelligence](../emergence/emergence-origin-intelligence.md), [social computation](../identity/the-non-individual-intelligence.md)); what this note adds is only the spine reading — composition as a way new generators enter the world **without being searched for**, which would bear on the family-search wall if it ever became measurable here.

Kept strictly apart: self-replication and takeover are *dynamical* phenomena. They demonstrate generator emergence; they say nothing about experience, and this note draws no line from one to the other.

## Boundary / non-claims

- No claim that BFF-style experiments prove digital life in general, machine consciousness, or AI personhood — they show self-replicators emerging in specific computational substrates, full stop.
- No claim that any current AI system is conscious; consciousness material stays in its two nodes.
- No claim that the ladder is a theory: it is a placement of existing formalisms, useful exactly insofar as it prevents category errors (measuring a trace and calling it a generator; possessing a description and calling it a process).
- Shannon, Kolmogorov, and Bateson are not graded from worse to better. Each is the right tool for its question.

## Open questions

1. **Can generator composition be measured in-repo?** A minimal testbed — two toy generators whose coupling produces a trace family neither produces alone, with the benchmark's equivalence-class machinery quantifying "higher-order" — would move the composition bridge from `[SPECULATIVE]` toward the lab. Unbuilt; candidate for the benchmark roadmap.
2. **Does the Bateson rung reduce without remainder to intervention-identifiability** (the regime hierarchy), or does "difference that makes a difference" carry content beyond it? If it fully reduces, the term is an external anchor rather than a new concept — worth knowing either way, and decidable by use.
3. **Valence stays outside.** Whether self-binding requires a valence-like signal is untouched by this repository's instruments; the term has no home here and is deliberately not introduced (registry rule: a term with no layer, home, experiment, or open problem is decoration — this entry is its open problem, and no more).
