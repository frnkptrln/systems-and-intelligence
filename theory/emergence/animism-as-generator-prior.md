# Animism as a Generator Prior (Framing Note)

**Status:** Working Note

**Scope:** Animism read as a stance on the inverse problem — a prior over generators that resolves trace→generator ambiguity toward *agents*. A claim about minds and inference, explicitly **not** about the cosmos, and explicitly **not** a one-paragraph theory of indigenous lifeworlds.

**Epistemic status:** `[HYPOTHESIZED]` framing over established cognitive-science anchors (agency detection, anthropomorphism theory, the intentional stance). The connection to this repository's measured Occam results is the note's contribution.

**Failure conditions:**

- Sliding from "animism is a prior in minds" to "the universe is animate." The first is testable cognitive science; the second is a narrative-layer statement and must be tagged as such wherever it appears.
- Treating animism *only* as error. A prior is not a bug; its value is world-dependent — that is this note's actual point.
- Colonizing anthropology: lived animist traditions are relational practices and ethics, not merely inference engines. This note addresses one face — the inferential one — and stops there.

## The claim

Every observer facing a trace must select from the equivalence class of generators consistent with it. Selection requires a prior. The [family-search testbed](../../lab/benchmarks/inverse-reconstruction/README.md) measured one such prior — simplicity — and found its value is world-dependent: reliable where the world is simple, chance where it is uniform, systematically wrong where it is complex.

**Animism is another prior over the same class: the agent prior** `[HYPOTHESIZED]`. Where Occam resolves ambiguity toward the *simplest* consistent generator, animism resolves it toward an *intentional* one — a generator with goals, perspective, and the capacity to respond. The rustle in the grass becomes a predator; the storm becomes an actor; the river becomes someone. Animism, on this reading, is not a primitive mistake about the world but a **systematic answer to the inverse problem**, applied with maximal scope.

## The anchors

- **Agency detection** (Barrett's HADD, 2004): humans run a *hyperactive* agent detector because the error costs are asymmetric — a false positive (fleeing a rustle that was wind) is cheap; a false negative (ignoring a rustle that was a leopard) is fatal. The detector is tuned hot by design, not by failure.
- **Anthropomorphism as strategy** (Guthrie, *Faces in the Clouds*, 1993): perceiving agents is the highest-stakes bet available to a perceiver — if right, it captures the most consequential structure in the environment; if wrong, it usually costs little.
- **The intentional stance** (Dennett, 1987): treating a system *as an agent* is a predictive strategy, justified by its compression payoff, not by metaphysics. Animism is the intentional stance with its scope dial turned to maximum.

In the benchmark's terms: the agent prior pays in worlds dense with agents — and the human world *is* dense with agents (other people are true positives, and they are the most important generators anyone ever reconstructs). It over-extends exactly where the Occam prior under-extends: on traces produced by blind dynamics that *look* purposive. Emergence is the great generator of such traces — flocks, markets, storms — which is why an emergence-rich world keeps animism permanently plausible: **self-organization is what agency looks like from outside, before the inverse problem is solved.**

## Two mirrors

- **Morelli, reversed.** Connoisseurship ([Art and Science](../narrative/art-science-one-practice-two-referees.md)) is *disciplined* generator-attribution — querying the incidental traces where the true hand leaks through. Animism is the same inference with the threshold removed: attribution without the discipline of divergence queries. Same machinery, different calibration — which is why the cure for over-attribution is not less inference but *better queries*.
- **The antenna, internalized.** The [psychedelics note](../identity/psychedelics-as-perturbation.md) describes mental events arriving untagged when the self-model's authorship-binding relaxes — and untagged traces trigger exactly this prior: a generator is inferred, and since it isn't *me*, it must be *someone*. Brain-as-antenna cosmologies are the agent prior applied to one's own thoughts, displaced outward. Animism begins at home.

## The boundary

As a claim about *minds* — that humans carry a hot agent prior, that it is ecologically rational, that its failure modes are measurable — this is ordinary, well-anchored cognitive science, and the repo's machinery adds a precise frame for it (priors over equivalence classes; world-dependent payoff; divergence queries as the calibration instrument).

As a claim about *the world* — that rivers, storms, or the universe are in fact agents or conscious — it is a different kind of statement: not falsifiable from traces alone (the equivalence class never collapses without divergence queries, and the cosmos affords none), and therefore at home only in the **narrative layer**, where it has always done its real work: as relational practice, as ethics, as story. That sorting is not a demotion; it is the assignment of the correct referee.
