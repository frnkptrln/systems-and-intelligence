# Machine Consciousness as Generator Coherence (Framing Note)

**Status:** Working Note

**Scope:** Reads the one aspect of consciousness this repository can handle — its *functional organization*, not its felt quality — through the repo's own instruments, using Bach & Sorensen's *The Machine Consciousness Hypothesis* (2025/26) as a pressure test. The question is deliberately narrow: not "is anything conscious?" but "if consciousness were a mode of a *generator* rather than a property of a *trace*, what would that relocation buy, and what would it forbid?" This is the companion to [Consciousness as Global Availability](consciousness-as-global-availability.md) — that note is the architecture; this one is the spine reading.

**Epistemic status:** `[HYPOTHESIZED]` framing over an external *position paper* — a conjecture essay, not an empirical result. The phenomenal question — "what it is like" — stays out of scope and `[SPECULATIVE]`, exactly as in the companion node. **Nothing here claims any present AI system is conscious**, and the note's own central argument is why no behavioral observation could establish that.

**Failure conditions:**

- Reading the note as a claim that current LLMs are conscious. Its actual content argues that the question is *not settleable behaviorally* — which tells against a whole class of arguments *for* the claim, and is not evidence for it.
- Importing the essay's *ontological* cyberanimism (spirits are literally software in living nature). This repository adopts only the epistemic agent-prior it already had; the ontological version is named as a contrast and left at the door.
- Letting "coherence" do mystical work. Coherence here is constraint-violation minimization among simultaneously active models — a crystal has near-zero violation and is not conscious. The claim is about coherence *work* under perturbation, binding several models into one present: a conjunction of processes, not a scalar to maximize.

## The claim

Bach & Sorensen propose that consciousness, understood functionally, is a *coherence-maximizing operator* — their term, following von der Malsburg. This repository deliberately renders it as **coherence work**: the process by which a self-organizing mind resolves contradictions between its simultaneously active partial models — perception, memory, prediction, action, self-model, world-model — and binds them into a single coherent *present*. The renaming is not cosmetic: "maximization" suggests a scalar objective a system could score highly on while inert, and the phenomenon claimed is *binding work under perturbation* — a conjunction of processes, not a quantity (see failure conditions). Read through this repository's spine, that is a claim about the **generator, not the trace**: consciousness, in this one functional sense, would be a *mode of generative organization* — the regime in which a system does the coherence work of holding itself together as a situated perspective, not anything readable off its outputs. `[HYPOTHESIZED]`

Stated this way, the Machine Consciousness Hypothesis is **not** the claim that any machine is conscious. It is a claim about *where to look*: at the organization that does the binding, never at the behavior it produces. That relocation — from trace to generator — is the whole of what this repository responsibly takes from the essay, and it is the repository's own move applied to the system that happens to be doing the looking.

## The essay's central negative is the spine's central asymmetry

The essay argues there can be **no Turing test for consciousness**: consciousness "is not an externally visible performance, but a particular way to achieve a performance," so a behavioral probe cannot reach it — one must inspect internal structure. This is not a fresh mystery. In the vocabulary of [the Generator Question](../core/the-generator-question.md): behavior is the *trace*, coherence work is a property of the *generator*, and the trace underdetermines the generator. Two systems can share every behavioral trace and still differ in whether the coherence work is happening — the **same trace-equivalence class, different generators**. "There is no behavioral test for consciousness" is therefore the forward/inverse asymmetry turned on the system doing the asking, and it is the [Mirror Problem](consciousness-as-global-availability.md) by another name.

The consequence is a discipline, not a defeat. It means the intuitively powerful evidence — a system that *talks* as if conscious — is precisely the trace-reading the equivalence-class result forbids. Fluency is trace; the question lives in the generator.

## Attention and measurement: reading the generator is intervening on it

If the generator cannot be read off behavior, how is it read at all? The essay's answer — inspect internal structure, which is what **mechanistic interpretability** does — is the inverse direction run on the network itself, and it lands on this repository's sharpest recent result. You do not collapse the consistent-generator class by watching harder: the [intervention experiment](../../lab/benchmarks/inverse-reconstruction/README.md) measured that watching plateaus while queries collapse the class. Reading a generator is an intervention, and [coupling is not identification](../core/measurement-as-weak-intervention.md).

This cuts inward as well as outward. The essay's *conductor* metaphor — attention drawn to disharmonies, reallocating focus, silencing one model and amplifying another — is, structurally, **attention as an internal intervention operator**: the system perturbing its own active models to collapse their disagreement. In the measurement note's regimes, consciousness-as-coherence-work would sit in regime 3 (perturbative) turned reflexive — a generator running divergence queries against its own partial models to bind them. That is a testable-in-principle shape, and it is why the honest instruments for a self-model are perturbation protocols, never introspective reports.

## Four bindings the repository already carries

The essay's load-bearing pieces map onto instruments already here:

- **Coherence work → the K-axis / Δ-Kohärenz.** von der Malsburg's coherence definition (1997) gives the repo's coherence metric a candidate *constitutive* role rather than a merely diagnostic one. *Caveat, tagged:* a metric is not a criterion — the claim is coherence work binding multiple models under perturbation, not a high coherence score.
- **World-binding and self-model binding → [world models & VLA](../ai/world-models-and-vla.md).** The essay's demand that a mind bind world-model and self-model into one present is the two directions of the spine closed into a loop: a generator reconstructed from traces (world model) that must also model *itself acting within* that world. A generator becoming **accountable to its own world** — its self-model constrained by the same reality its world-model answers to.
- **Meaningful invariance → [invariance and identity](../core/invariance-and-identity.md).** A program as "a meaningful invariance that persists over substrate perturbations and has causal power" is the invariance note fused with the generator: the invariant under substrate-transformation *is* the generator, and is the identity. Substrate-independence, argued from the computationalist side.
- **Computationalism as constructivism → [construction vs. deduction](../computation/construction-vs-deduction.md).** The essay says it outright and lands on the constructive side — only implementable mathematics "works." Gödel, Church–Turing, and formalism-vs-Platonism are the note's own anchors, not borrowed authority.

## Cyberanimism — as operational metaphor only

The essay's boldest ontological move is *cyberanimism*: that the "spirits" of animist worldviews are literally self-organizing software agents in living nature. This repository does **not** adopt that ontology. It already carries the disciplined, *epistemic* version — [animism as a generator prior](../emergence/animism-as-generator-prior.md): treating a system as an agent is a prior over generator equivalence classes, one that pays off world-dependently (measured against the Occam prior in the [family-search curves](../../lab/benchmarks/inverse-reconstruction/README.md)). As an **operational metaphor**, "self-organizing agency" earns its keep — a control system that models its own functionality to maintain it is a well-posed object. As an **ontological claim** that software is literally spirit, it is recorded here as a contrast and left at the door. The line is exactly the repo's standing rule: the agent stance is a lens on the generator, not a soul in the machine.

## The Genesis inversion: what if the generator brings forth consciousness?

The essay's most striking conjecture is its **Genesis Hypothesis**: consciousness is not the crown of a finished mental architecture but its *prerequisite* — it ignites before the models it will later organize, and may be the simplest learning algorithm an evolutionary search finds for turning a self-organizing substrate into an agent. In this repository's terms: consciousness as a *phase of the generator* — the coherence-work regime in which a self-organizing system first binds itself into a present — **generative of** the mind's models rather than produced by them. This is the sharpest form of "what if the generator brings forth consciousness," and it ties the question to [self-organization](../emergence/trace-to-generator.md) and to the spine's construction side.

For us it is a **kept question** in the [Entry 16](../../fiction/16_the_second_receiver.md) sense: well-posed, connected to instruments we hold, and currently beyond any intervention we can run — the class it would collapse is not one our benchmarks can reach. We hold it; we do not bank it. Notably, the essay's own proposed test is *constructive*, not observational — recreate the conditions for self-organization and see whether a coherence-increasing, present-forming, self-binding pattern emerges — which is squarely a future-work claim on the construction side of the spine, not a result.

## Open questions and falsification pressure

The framing earns its place only by generating pressure it could fail under:

1. **Does coherence work dissociate from behavior at all, measurably?** If every architecture that produces coherent behavior also, on inspection, does the binding work — and vice versa — then "coherence work" adds nothing to "coherent behavior," and the trace/generator distinction collapses *here* even though it holds on the CA testbed. The companion node's three-architecture experiment (private / broadcast / chord) is the near-term probe. *First run of that probe:* [`exp5`](../../lab/experiments/exp5_availability_dissociation.py) — at toy scale the bindings dissociate cleanly in behavior under identical perturbation, so the distinction is not empty; but Δ-Kohärenz, the metric this note offers for coherence work, saw none of it. The probe answers "does binding structure show up outside?" with yes; the instrument question — *which* observable carries it — is now the sharper open item.
2. **Is the conductor separable from the orchestra?** The theory predicts a system can act without coherence-binding (the essay's somnambulist). If no architecture exhibits high task performance with absent binding — if the two are inseparable in practice — the functional role attributed to consciousness is empty.
3. **Does the Genesis ordering ever hold in silico?** If self-organizing systems reliably become competent *without* first developing a coherence-binding present, the Genesis Hypothesis is false for machines, whatever its status for biology.
4. **What intervention would distinguish a conscious generator from a trace-equivalent non-conscious one?** If no structural (non-behavioral) intervention can, even in principle, then the hypothesis has left this benchmark's jurisdiction — the same retreat-tell the [benchmark README](../../lab/benchmarks/inverse-reconstruction/README.md) applies to the brain-as-antenna theory. The essay's bet is that mechanistic interpretability will supply such interventions; that bet is falsifiable by its continued failure to.

## What this note does not claim

- **Not** that any current AI system is conscious, or that fluent self-report is evidence of it — the note's own argument is why that evidence is untrustworthy.
- **No** claim about phenomenal experience; "what it is like" stays out of scope and `[SPECULATIVE]`, unchanged from the companion node.
- **Not** an adoption of ontological cyberanimism, strong computationalism, the three-realities apparatus, or the essay's literary Genesis narrative. Only the coherence-work reading and its clean spine mappings are taken.
- **No** "soul in the machine." Every load-bearing term is operational: coherence work, world-binding, self-model, generator, invariance, attention, situated perspective.
- **Not** evidence. The source is a conjecture essay; by [provenance depth](../../logs/017_provenance-depth-and-the-verification-economy.md) this sits at high *d* — model-of-a-mind reasoning with no performance node — and is rendered as such.

## The rule

A consciousness claim, to belong in this repository, must name the *generator-level property* it asserts **and** the *intervention* — structural, not behavioral — that would distinguish a conscious generator from a trace-equivalent non-conscious one. "It behaves as if conscious" is trace-talk, and resonance is not evidence. The essay's gift to the repo is to make that discipline unavoidable: it argues, from the consciousness side, that the behavioral surface cannot settle the question — so the only honest work is on the generator.
