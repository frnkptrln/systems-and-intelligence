# Part 1: The Mechanics of Emergence

Before we can build metrics for intelligence, we must understand how structure arises from chaos. 

The core thesis of this repository is that **the same constraints repeat on every scale — not by analogy, but by necessity.** This is the [Fractal Architecture of Emergence](../../theory/fractal-architecture-of-emergence.md).

## Local Causality, Invisible Consequences
The defining feature of complex systems is that the agents executing the rules have no access to the global state they are producing. A bird in a flock (Boids) does not know the shape of the flock. An oscillator (Kuramoto) does not know the phase of the aggregate. 

This is explored deeply in [Local Causality and Invisible Consequences](../../theory/local-causality-invisible-consequences.md). It is the structural commonality of every simulation in this repository.

## The Phase Transition to Intelligence
Intelligence is not programmed; it is phase-transitioned. The [Grokking Phase Transition](../../theory/grokking-phase-transition.md) simulation demonstrates how systems shift from memorization to generalization, a thermodynamic collapse into a lower entropy state.

## Autopoiesis: Self-Sustaining Information
Finally, we model intelligence as life itself. The **Lenia** ecosystem models continuous cellular automata. It shows how boundaries form, how systems self-repair, and how "agents" emerge from a continuous field of rules.

*To see these mechanics in action, run the [Phase Transition Explorer](../../simulation-models/README.md) or explore the dynamical simulations directly.*
