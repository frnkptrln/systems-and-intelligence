# Thinking Space

**Status:** Open exploration — questions and fragments on this page are not repository claims.

**Scope:** A place for encounters that may not yet have a stable name, model, home document, or
failure condition. The Thinking Space is not an intake queue for the canonical synthesis.

This is where the repository is allowed not to know.

Executable probes, architecture logs, theoretical fragments, fiction, contradictions, and
unfinished design moves can remain here without promising that they will mature into theory. Some
will become experiments or open problems. Some will be revised beyond recognition. Some may remain
useful questions indefinitely.

---

## Working contract

- **A question may precede a claim.** It does not need a mechanism before it can be recorded.
- **Contradictions may remain visible.** Premature reconciliation can erase the anomaly that made
  the encounter interesting.
- **Classification is optional here.** A seed does not need a canonical home, concept-registry row,
  or maturity tag merely to exist.
- **Promotion is not the purpose.** Remaining unresolved is a legitimate outcome.
- **Epistemic honesty still applies.** Sources should be named, speculation must not present itself
  as evidence, and Thinking Space must not silently strengthen a canonical claim.

The distinction is temporal as well as structural:

| Clock | Work | Required discipline |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **Encounter** | Notice a result, image, contradiction, analogy, or question | Preserve what is surprising; state what must not yet be concluded |
| **Investigation** | Build an argument, comparison, model, or intervention | Declare assumptions, alternatives, and failure conditions |
| **Synthesis** | Compress a result into the maintained claim structure | Assign a home, status, evidence path, and scope boundary |

These clocks need not run in the same commit. Latency between them protects the project from
translating every new encounter immediately into language it already knows.

## Blips: the smallest unit

The raw note layer lives in [`ideas/`](https://github.com/frnkptrln/systems-and-intelligence/tree/main/ideas).
A **Blip** holds one observation, question, hypothesis, analogy, or disturbance — usually in about
100–200 words — before it needs a category or destination. Links may accumulate; classification does
not have to come first.

The Thinking Space remains the wider map of exploratory work. It can surface selected Blips,
contradictions, experiments, logs, or fiction without turning the Blip collection into a queue for
canonical promotion.

## Questions before concepts

These selected seeds are deliberately not additional claims or numbered open problems.

### J-space and JEPA: availability is not prediction

- **Encounter:** The [J-space result](theory/ai/j-space-and-global-availability.md) reports a bounded,
  broadcast-coupled internal representation space; [JEPA and related world models](theory/ai/world-models-and-vla.md)
  organize predictive representations without making the same workspace claim.
- **Disturbance:** Reading J-space only through the existing global-availability vocabulary may
  assimilate the result too quickly. Predictive adequacy and broad internal availability may be
  independent architectural axes.
- **Question:** Can a system have a rich predictive latent space without workspace-style broadcast,
  or broadcast without a useful world model? Which observation instrument would distinguish them?
- **Do not conclude yet:** that J-space and JEPA name the same object, that either is necessary for
  intelligence, or that workspace-like function establishes experience.

### After the generator: what brings structure forth?

- **Encounter:** The [Foundations Reconstruction](theory/core/mathematical-axioms.md) found no
  independent mathematical role for one unqualified “generator”; update rules, state, environment,
  runtime, history, and observation belong to a typed process description.
- **Disturbance:** Decomposing the old word corrects the mathematics without exhausting the older
  question. It remains meaningful to ask why some processes form, persist, recur, or become
  explanatory while others do not.
- **Question:** After the bundle is decomposed, what explanatory remainder—selection, constraint,
  symmetry breaking, boundary formation, observer-relative compression, or something else—accounts
  for stable form?
- **Do not conclude yet:** that this remainder requires a new primitive or restores the legacy
  generator as a universal object.

### Who pays for viability?

- **Encounter:** Benchmark v1.13 and [Log 019](logs/019_who_pays_for_the_veto.md) show that relocating
  the cost of support from an individual donor to its local group nearly removes selection against
  support and suppresses seeded cheaters, while reducing population size.
- **Disturbance:** The topology of payment may matter as much as the nominal constraint. A mechanism
  can be collectively useful yet unstable when its carriers alone bear its cost; removing that
  penalty is still not evidence that the trait is positively selected.
- **Question:** What intervention separates selected retention from neutral drift, and when does
  cost relocation preserve a function rather than merely change who disappears?
- **Do not conclude yet:** that the toy result transfers to institutions, governance, biological
  cooperation, or real AI-agent systems.

## Optional maturation

An exploratory artifact **may** move toward synthesis when it has:

1. a precise claim,
2. a bounded model or argument,
3. a failure condition,
4. links to relevant evidence and alternatives,
5. an explicit statement of what it does not show.

These conditions protect the synthesis layer. They do not define the value of exploration, and
nothing in the Thinking Space owes the repository a promotion.
